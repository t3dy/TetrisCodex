## Motif Extraction

### Ubik

QueryPat archive metadata emphasizes half-life states, temporal regression, entropy, degradation, commercialized psychic labor, and unreliable reality. Exegesis/AIpassages references add a useful design gloss: Ubik as anti-entropy, medicine, antidote, salvific information, and intrusion from a more real level.

Balance Tetris translation:

- Entropy becomes block rot, mass drift, color fading, support weakening, and platform regression.
- Ubik becomes a rare restorative spray, signal, beam, or consumable that re-solidifies corrupted cells.
- Half-life becomes phase-shifting support: a block may still weigh on the platform while temporarily failing to support stacked pieces.

### A Scanner Darkly

QueryPat archive metadata for the screenplay/adaptation points to Bob Arctor, a hallucinatory drug underworld, surveillance, perceptual unreliability, and identity dissolution. AIpassages also links Scanner to split-brain/two-minded perception and occlusion.

Balance Tetris translation:

- The current piece can be visually scrambled: shown as one shape, physically resolved as another.
- Controls can split into two competing mappings: left half obeys one scheme, right half another.
- Scanner tools reveal hidden mass, friction, or allegiance for only a few turns.

### The Man In The High Castle

QueryPat archive metadata emphasizes an alternate-history America, divided jurisdictions, and the I Ching guiding both characters and authorial structure.

Balance Tetris translation:

- Each move branches between two physics timelines.
- A hexagram roll/draw selects a rule modifier before placement.
- Some pieces exist in one timeline but cast weight into the other.

### Time Out Of Joint

QueryPat archive metadata describes Ragle Gumm gradually discovering that his small-town reality is a constructed illusion.

Balance Tetris translation:

- The platform begins as a stable, friendly tabletop but sheds fake scenery as play proceeds.
- Blocks, UI labels, and scoring targets are revealed to be stage-set substitutions.
- A late-level reality-collapse event recalculates all hidden weights.

### Minority Report

The QueryPat archive entry for the story collection includes "The Minority Report" among classic stories. The usable motif is precognition under institutional pressure: several futures are visible, but preventing one future may privilege or suppress another.

Balance Tetris translation:

- The next three danger states are forecast.
- The player can cancel one future piece or one future torque event.
- A minority forecast may contradict the visible queue and be correct only under certain physics settings.

### Do Androids Dream Of Electric Sheep?

Structured archive material identifies the Androids text/adaptation cluster; QueryPat also includes "The Android and the Human." The usable motif is empathy, artificial life, moral agency, and indistinguishable entities.

Balance Tetris translation:

- Empathy links blocks: supporting one block can stabilize a distant partner.
- Synthetic blocks look like living/organic blocks but differ in mass, brittleness, or scoring.
- Kipple accumulates as junk debris that fills gaps, adds nuisance mass, and resists clearing.

### VALIS

QueryPat archive metadata frames VALIS and the trilogy around theology, Gnosticism, divine reality, revelation, and the 1974 experience material.

Balance Tetris translation:

- A VALIS beam reveals hidden structure or turns invisible physics values into visible glyphs.
- Gnostic prison blocks lock regions until hit by information/light.
- Anamnesis becomes a board reveal: what looked random is recategorized as a pattern.

### The Three Stigmata Of Palmer Eldritch

Found through the five-novel omnibus entry and related PKD archive material. The useful design motif is nested fantasy, unstable substitution, colonist escapism, and invasive external agency.

Balance Tetris translation:

- A tempting alternate board offers easier placements but contaminates the real board when the player returns.
- Three-marked blocks spread a property to neighbors: delusion, ownership, viscosity, or false support.
- Level success asks whether the player can exploit illusion without letting it own the whole stack.

### The Days Of Perky Pat

Found via the Minority Report collection metadata. The usable motif is miniature domestic play as compensation for devastated reality.

Balance Tetris translation:

- Dollhouse/furniture tiles must be arranged on a tiny platform while outside shocks disturb it.
- Decorative comfort blocks score, but too much comfort makes the stack top-heavy.
- Real-world "basement" pressure periodically tilts the miniature world.

### Electric Ant

Exegesis references around internal tape, clock, and dysynchronization provide the strongest QueryPat anchor. The usable motif is editable reality substrate.

Balance Tetris translation:

- A moving tape at the bottom of the UI defines gravity, friction, and wind over time.
- The player can punch or splice the tape to alter physics.
- Deleting tape sections can delete future supports or skip future hazards.

### A Maze Of Death

Structured archive entry found. The usable motif is incomplete cosmology, unreliable group reality, and possible synthetic/shared world.

Balance Tetris translation:

- Multiple agents or gods each expose only part of the platform rules.
- The board periodically fuses partial realities and recalculates mass.
- Co-op or hot-seat players may each see different block properties.

### Clans Of The Alphane Moon

Structured archive entry found. The useful motif is factional psychotypes and social ordering by mental state.

Balance Tetris translation:

- Each block class has a temperament: manic pieces slide fast, paranoid pieces avoid adjacency, depressive pieces get heavy, obsessive pieces lock orientation, healer pieces stabilize neighbors.
- Levels are faction puzzles: build a coalition stack from contradictory behaviors.

### Dr. Bloodmoney

Structured archive entry found. The useful motif is post-bomb survival, mutation, distributed power, and altered bodies/voices.

Balance Tetris translation:

- Radiation weather mutates block mass, slipperiness, or shape after placement.
- Mutant blocks can support remotely, repel neighbors, or split.
- Fallout wind and ash add persistent load to one side of the platform.

## Implementation Bias

The most buildable first passes are the modes that reuse existing Balance Tetris systems:

1. **Ubik Anti-Entropy**: needs block aging, one restorative action, and visual decay.
2. **Minority Report Queue**: needs richer queue preview and discard/cancel verbs.
3. **Scanner Scramble**: needs hidden true properties and reveal overlay.
4. **High Castle Hexagrams**: needs a pre-turn modifier draw and dual-rule level config.
5. **Androids/Kipple**: needs nuisance debris and linked block scoring.

The later, more expensive modes are those requiring parallel timelines, multi-agent perspectives, or board-wide reality rewrites.
