# Tarot Quiz Variants

The tarot quiz system should support several kinds of interpretive play, from softball tutorials to higher-risk scoring puzzles.

## Variant 1: Direct Correspondence Quiz

Question:

```text
Four of Wands followed by Five of Wands most obviously suggests:
```

Choices:

- development of will
- deepening knowledge
- emotional stability increasing

Purpose:

- teach suit grammar
- make some answers obviously wrong
- build confidence

## Variant 2: Trump Scale Quiz

Question:

```text
A Trump usually points first toward:
```

Choices:

- forces outside the querent's control
- the user discovers superpowers
- a small daily mood

Purpose:

- teach card class scale
- distinguish Major/Trump archetypal pressure from wish fulfillment

## Variant 3: Frame-Steering Quiz

Question:

```text
You chose the Career frame. The Moon appears. How do you keep the interpretation useful?
```

Choices:

- translate uncertainty into timing and hidden information
- pretend the Moon means guaranteed promotion
- ignore the frame entirely

Purpose:

- let players steer toward career, relationships, study, or inner work
- score higher if the interpretation respects both card and frame

## Variant 4: Correspondence Spend Quiz

Question:

```text
You have one Mars coin and one Water coin. Which connection deserves attention?
```

Choices:

- Mars on Tower rupture
- Water on Tower emotional comfort
- Mars on Ace of Cups romance

Purpose:

- tie quiz choices to attention coins
- make correspondence literacy an economy

## Variant 5: Dazzle Chain Quiz

Question:

```text
The hand triggers 5, Wands, Mars, and Fire. What kind of chain is this?
```

Choices:

- conflict/will/heat chain
- knowledge/air/speech chain
- emotional stability/water chain

Purpose:

- drive Balatro-like combo recognition
- make scoring fireworks explainable

## Variant 6: Source-Labeled Quiz

Question:

```text
This attribution comes from a Golden Dawn-style system. What should the UI do?
```

Choices:

- label the tradition and allow alternatives
- pretend all tarot traditions agree
- hide the source

Purpose:

- teach historiographic caution
- keep the game honest while still playful

## Variant 7: Wrong-But-Playable Quiz

Question:

```text
The player wants a Relationships reading from a severe Mars/Saturn hand. What is the least bad move?
```

Choices:

- read it as boundary, pressure, repair, or conflict management
- declare it romantic bliss
- abandon the spread

Purpose:

- allow risky translations without making every non-obvious answer fail
- support creative play
