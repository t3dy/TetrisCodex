# Alchemical History By Game Mode

This commentary explains how each game mode can explore alchemical history and chemistry while using exaggerated or fudged physics for playability.

## Design Principle

The game should be honest about its fictions. A block may bounce, explode, slide, or transform faster than any real material, but the source frame should explain the historical idea being dramatized.

## Balance Tetris

Historical focus:

- weight, fixity, volatility
- metallic/planetary correspondences
- projection and transmutation
- controlled tipping into receivers as sorting/refining

Example level:

- Lead/Saturn blocks are heavy and sink the platform.
- The player prepares a Projector/Stone apparatus.
- Success transforms a Saturn mass into Sun/gold score.

Gameplay fudge:

- Real alchemical projection was not a one-click side dump.
- The mode uses torque and receivers to make "proper destination" playable.

## Rune Bloom

Historical focus:

- growth, generation, corruption, purification
- emblematic gardens and vegetative metaphors
- fermentation and multiplication

Gameplay fudge:

- Blooming runes are symbolic, not literal laboratory chemistry.

## Alchemy Snake

Historical focus:

- ingestion/digestion as transformation
- medicine, pharmacy, poison, antidote
- Ouroboros symbolism
- powers gained from substances

Gameplay fudge:

- Eating Mercury grants ghost movement; this is a playful metaphor for volatility.

## Alchemical Three

Historical focus:

- matter handled through tools, labor, and apparatus
- fixity, volatility, heat, and specialized craft knowledge

Gameplay fudge:

- Characters embody principles more cleanly than historical workers would.

## VALIS Space Taxi

Historical focus:

- revelation, hidden properties, unstable perception
- source criticism as "beam reveal"
- alchemical cargo as dangerous matter

Gameplay fudge:

- PKDian revelation beams are speculative overlays, but they model hidden nature and disclosure.

## Wild Hunt

Historical focus:

- Trithemius, learned historiography, legend, chronology, perception
- the boundary between occult report and scholarly framing

Gameplay fudge:

- Folkloric apparitions become blocks with mass and omen effects.

## Alchemical Billiards

Historical focus:

- apparatus collision as operation
- reagent routing
- cascade accident and controlled procedure

Gameplay fudge:

- Pool-ball physics turns complex operations into visible collisions.

## Alchemical Breakout

Historical focus:

- repeated operation, heat, residue, purification
- breaking down matter through cycles

Gameplay fudge:

- Bricks stand in for materials and vessels.

## Alchemical Push

Historical focus:

- workshop manipulation
- heavy matter, brittle glassware, wet floors, furnaces

Gameplay fudge:

- Sokoban movement turns lab logistics into single-tile pushes.

## Lab Minesweeper

Historical focus:

- danger, hidden properties, experiment risk, inference
- reading signs before acting

Gameplay fudge:

- Minesweeper numbers simplify complex diagnostic knowledge.

## Medieval Siege Alchemy

Historical focus:

- fire, gunpowder-adjacent craft, metallurgy, corrosion, engineering
- practical alchemy in war and medicine

Gameplay fudge:

- Bombs, ice spells, and flamethrowers are exaggerated action interfaces.

## Design Rule

Each mode's side frame should include:

- historical concept
- source/person/text
- gameplay translation
- what is exaggerated
- one question to notice while playing
