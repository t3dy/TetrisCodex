# Tarot Tetris Symbol Stacks

Tarot Tetris turns each card into a stack or piece made from its attributions. A tarot card is not one block. It is a bundle of symbol blocks: number, suit, element, planet, zodiac, Hebrew letter, path, process, and source-tradition labels.

## User Prompt

The user proposed:

- tarot Tetris where the number of bricks is determined by the card's "size"
- Trumps are bigger than court cards, court cards bigger than pips in power/score/size
- a 10 of Wands piece could contain:
  - a number 10 block
  - a Wands/fire block
  - planetary block
  - zodiacal block
  - elemental block
- blocks should visibly translate esoteric reasoning into mechanics
- attributes should push, attract, react, and clear lines
- opportunistic associations could trigger effects like earthquakes

## Piece Scale

| Card Type | Piece Size | Interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pip | 3-5 cells | ordinary symbolic matter |
| Court | 5-7 cells | personified agency |
| Trump | 7-12 cells | archetypal mass |

Alternative sizing:

- Pip number determines cell count directly, but capped or compressed
- Trump number determines score multiplier, not physical size
- Courts occupy humanoid/tall pieces
- Trumps arrive as boss pieces with several attached symbols

## Clearing Rules

### Line Clear

Traditional row clear remains the basic readable goal.

### Correspondence Clear

A group clears if it forms a meaningful symbolic set:

- all four elements
- 3 Mothers
- 7 Doubles
- 12 Singles
- repeated number ladder
- planet + matching Trump
- sign + ruler + element

### Interpretation Clear

The player answers a prompt:

```text
This row has Fire, Saturn, and 10. What is the best interpretation?
```

Correct answer triggers a clear or multiplier.

## Variants

1. **Symbol Stack Falling Blocks**: card appears as a vertical stack of symbols.
2. **Card Polyominoes**: each card generates a shaped piece from attribution density.
3. **Trump Boss Drops**: Trumps drop as huge unstable archetypal pieces.
4. **Court Helpers**: court cards become characters that rotate or move pieces.
5. **Spread Board Tetris**: positions in a spread are wells or scoring lanes.
6. **Correspondence Cascade Mode**: focus on dazzling automatic symbolic reactions.
7. **Quiz-Gated Clears**: clears require choosing a faithful interpretation.

## Design Goal

The player should be overwhelmed at first by symbol stacks, then gradually realize that the symbols are not arbitrary decorations. They are tiny engines. Fire wants to burn, Saturn wants to weigh, Mercury wants to swap, and the board becomes delightful because correspondences cause readable chaos.
